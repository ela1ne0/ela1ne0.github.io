Nebraska Flood Awareness Initiative

Flood-risk information is often technically accurate but visually inaccessible. I led the design of public-facing materials that translated complex geospatial flood data into clear, approachable visuals for 8,000+ rural Nebraska residents.

RoleLead Designer
AudienceRural households
ToolsIllustrator, GIS exports
Reach8,000+ residents

Overview

The goal was clarity—not density. We needed to communicate flood risk in a way that felt readable, actionable, and non-alarmist for residents who may not be familiar with maps or technical GIS symbology.

The Challenge

Flood maps are built for accuracy, but they can be difficult for the public to interpret. Dense symbology, layered classifications, and small legends make it hard to understand what “risk” actually means for a home.

We needed to help residents quickly answer:

  • What zone am I in?
  • What does that mean for insurance and preparedness?
  • What should I do next?

Data Translation

I started by isolating the most essential layers and removing visual noise. Instead of presenting every classification, we grouped risk into clearer tiers and built a high-contrast legend designed for quick scanning.

Color choices were intentionally reduced to avoid overwhelming the viewer and to keep the print results consistent.

Postcard Design

The final deliverable was a double-sided postcard. The front provided a simplified visual overview of flood risk, and the back offered a short checklist of preparedness steps.

Typography was intentionally large. Icons were simplified. The tone stayed practical and calm—informative, not alarmist—because the objective was understanding and action.

Impact

  • Distributed to 8,000+ residents across rural Nebraska
  • Improved clarity of flood-risk communication for a non-technical audience
  • Created a repeatable outreach template for future campaigns

Reflection

This project reinforced that good design is often invisible. The best outcome wasn’t aesthetic praise—it was comprehension. Translating technical GIS outputs into actionable communication sharpened my ability to reduce complexity without losing meaning.
